What is a callsign?

Here, callsign refers to an amateur radio callsigns. This tool is designed for ham radio operators. Callsigns are (to large extent) formed according to the following structure:
FormatQuantityPart
Arabic numberat the beginning or not at allcountry prefix
Latin letter(s)at least onecountry prefix
Arabic numberone except for special callsignsnumber
Latin letter(s)mostly two or three letterssuffix
The meaning of each part differs across countries and is irrelevant here.

What is being analyzed?

The callsign is measured and classified using various metrics. These metrics are indicators that can hint at practical aspects, such as ease of spelling and ease of remembering.

How is it done?

We apply techniques from the field of Natural Language Processing (NLP). The callsign is not analyzed based on syntax alone. Rather, it is translated into a phonetic transcription. This allows us to provide pronunciation-related information as well.

Why was is done?

Callsigns can be a means of self-expression or of delivering a message, but they can also have negative side effects, both practically and socially.
